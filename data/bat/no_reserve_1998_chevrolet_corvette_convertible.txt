
This 1998 Chevrolet Corvette convertible was delivered new to Turnpike
Chevrolet in Nitro, West Virginia and is finished in red with a black soft
top over black leather. The car was originally purchased by the selling
dealer in 2007, sold in 2013, and reacquired from its sixth owner in July
2020. Power is provided by a 5.7-liter LS1 V8 paired with a four-speed
automatic transmission and a limited-slip differential. Modifications
include an BBK cold air intake, a Borla exhaust system, Z06-style wheels,
chrome roll hoops, a Kenwood touchscreen head unit, and a trunk-mounted
10'' subwoofer. This C5 convertible indicates 76k miles and is offered at
no reserve with a reproduction window sticker and build sheet,
manufacturer's literature, service records, a Carfax report, and a clean
Michigan title.

No Reserve: 1998 Chevrolet Corvette Convertible

The car is finished in Torch Red and features matching body-side moldings,
tinted taillight lenses, chrome roll hoops with integrated lighting, and a
black convertible top that can be seen both raised and lowered. The Carfax
report shows that an accident in occurred October 2001 with no further
information provided, while a rear impact in June 2007 is also reported
and the selling dealer notes that the rear bumper cover was repainted as a
result.


The C5 featured a hydroformed box frame, a first for the Corvette model.
Double-staggered Z06-style wheels measure 17a*^3 up front and 18a*^3 out
back and are fitted with Cooper RS3-S tires that show date codes from 2011
and 2018.


The cabin features power-adjustable sport seats trimmed in black leather,
an electric steering column, dual-zone automatic climate control, an
integrated radar detector, and a Kenwood touchscreen head unit. A 10''
subwoofer is mounted in the trunk.


Instrumentation consists of a 200-mph speedometer and a tachometer along
with gauges to monitor coolant temperature, oil pressure, voltage, and
fuel level. The odometer indicates 76k miles. The selling dealer notes
that the tire pressure monitoring system is inoperable.


The 5.7-liter LS1 V8 was factory-rated at 345 horsepower at 5,600 rpm and
350 lb-ft of torque at 4,400 rpm. Modifications include a BBK cold air
intake and Borla dual exhaust system. An oil and filter change was
completed in preparation for the sale.


Power is sent to the rear wheels through a rigid torque tube and a
rear-mounted four-speed automatic transaxle. Additional photos showing the
underside as well as corrosion on areas of the exhaust system and
