Jacobs has been signed to WWE since 1995 and was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in WWE Hall of Fame (2021)|2021. He has also been the Republican Party (United States)|Republican Mayor of Knox County, Tennessee since 2018. He began his professional wrestling career on the independent circuit in 1992, wrestling in promotions such as Smoky Mountain Wrestling (SMW) and the United States Wrestling Association (USWA), before joining the World Wrestling Federation (WWF, now WWE) in 1995. Jacobs played various Gimmick (professional wrestling)|characters until 1997, when he was repackaged as Kane, a Horror film|horror-themed personification of fire and juggernaut younger half-brother of The Undertaker and son of Paul Bearer. This "hellfire and brimstone" gimmick branches from acts of arson by The Undertaker as part of their characters' The Brothers of Destruction#Backstory and Kane's debut (1997–1998)|youth backstory. Jacobs would alternatively Feud (professional wrestling)|feud and Tag team|team with The Undertaker as The Brothers of Destruction. Following his WWF debut, Kane remained a pivotal component of the company's "Attitude Era" of the late 1990s and early 2000s, defeating the era's "poster boy" Stone Cold Steve Austin for the WWE Championship|WWF Championship in his first List of WWE pay-per-view events|pay-per-view (PPV) main event at King of the Ring (1998)|King of the Ring in June 1998. He continued to headline PPV cards through 2018's Crown Jewel (2018)|Crown Jewel, and has appeared in more PPVs than any other performer in WWF/WWE history other than Randy Orton. Within WWE, Kane is a three-time World championship (professional wrestling)|world champion (having held the WWF Championship, ECW World Heavyweight Championship|ECW Championship and World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Championship once each) and a 12-time World tag team championship|world tag team champion (having held the World Tag Team Championship (WWE)|World Tag Team Championship, WCW Tag Team Championship and WWE Raw Tag Team Championship|WWE Tag Team Championships with various partners). He is also a two-time List of WWE Intercontinental Champions|Intercontinental Champion and a Money in the Bank (2010)|Money in the Bank winner, as well as the third man to complete WWE's Grand Slam (professional wrestling)#WWE Grand Slam Champions|Grand Slam. The last title Kane won was the WWE 24/7 Championship|24/7 Championship on September 16, 2019, the only title won under his real name.  Veteran wrestler Ric Flair described Kane as "the best in the world", while Big Show dubbed him "the best big man ever". Kane holds the record for the most appearances in a Royal Rumble match at 20, as well as the highest cumulative total eliminations at 46. Outside of professional wrestling, Jacobs has made numerous guest appearances in film and on television, including the lead role in the 2006 WWE Studios production "See No Evil (2006 film)|See No Evil" and its See No Evil 2|2014 sequel. He is also a longtime supporter of Libertarianism in the United States|libertarian political causes. In March 2017, Jacobs announced that he was running for the mayoral seat of Knox County, Tennessee as a Republican. He won the Republican primary election on May 1, 2018, and the general election on August 2, 2018. He was re-elected as Knox County Mayor on August 4, 2022. == Early life == Jacobs was born in the Spanish town of Torrejón de Ardoz to a United States Air Force family that was stationed in Spain at the time of his birth. He grew up near St. Louis, Missouri, and attended high school in Bowling Green, Missouri, where he excelled in American football|football and basketball. He went on to earn a degree in English literature at Northeast Missouri State University, where he played both basketball and football. == Professional wrestling career ==   }}   Jacobs debuted in 1992 under the ring name "Angus King", wrestling for the Hannibal, Missouri-based Central States Wrestling Association (which was owned and run by his childhood friend Mark Morton). In December 1992, Jacobs appeared with the Memphis, Tennessee-based United States Wrestling Association (USWA) as "the Christmas Creature", an "evil Christmas character" who wore a garish costume (made by Jacobs' mother) featuring a green wrestling mask|mask, candy cane-striped sleeves, and tinsel. The Christmas Creature unsuccessfully challenged Jerry Lawler for the USWA Unified World Heavyweight Championship before leaving USWA later that month. In March 1993, Jacobs made a one-off appearance with World Championship Wrestling, losing to Sting (wrestler)|Sting on an episode of "WCW Saturday Night" filmed in Macon, Georgia, under the ring name "Bruiser Mastino". Later that year, he relocated to Tampa, Florida to further his training. During his time in Florida, he wrestled a handful of matches for the International Championship Wrestling Alliance as "Sid Powers". After leaving Florida, Jacobs returned to the USWA as "Doomsday". In October 10, 1993, Jacobs wrestled a dark match for the World Wrestling Federation under his real name defeating Mike Bell (wrestler)|Mike Bell at a "WWF Superstars of Wrestling|WWF Superstars" taping in Burlington, Vermont. In December 1993, Jacobs wrestled in Japan for Pro Wrestling Fujiwara Gumi under his real name. Jacobs began 1994 wrestling for the Central City, Kentucky-based Tri-State Wrestling promotion and the Indianapolis, Indiana-based Championship Wrestling from Indiana promotion as "Doomsday", as well as making a handful of further appearances with Pro Wrestling Fujiwara Gumi in Japan. In May 1994, Jacobs relocated to Puerto Rico to wrestle for the World Wrestling Council as "Doomsday", where he feuded with José González (wrestler)|Invader 1. In November and December 1994, Jacobs wrestled for the Catch Wrestling Association in Bremen, Germany as "Spartacus", competing in its Catch Cup tournament.
