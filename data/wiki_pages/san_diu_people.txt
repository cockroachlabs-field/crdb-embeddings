 <br />Người Sán Dìu () |total=183,004 |total_ref= |popplace= Northern Vietnam |rels=Predominantly Mahayana Buddhism<br> Taoism, Catholic Church|Catholicism and Evangelicalism |langs=Yue Chinese • Chinese language|Standard Chinese • Vietnamese language|Vietnamese • Pinghua |related=Hoa people|Chinese Vietnamese, Yao people }} The "Sán Dìu" (also known as "San Deo", "Trai", "Trai Dat" and "Man Quan Coc"; ; Chữ nôm: ; Vietnamese alphabet: Người Sán Dìu) are a Yao people|Yao ethnic group in northern Vietnam who speak Yue Chinese (Cantonese), a Sinitic languages|Sinitic language. They are believed to have migrated from Guangdong, China around 1600. The group's estimated population as of 2000 was 117,500; the 2019 census put the number at 183,004. They speak a variant of Cantonese, and it is suggested that some still speak Iu Mien language|Iu Mien. The major religions are Mahayana Buddhism and Taoism, with elements of animism and veneration of the dead.  About 400 are adherents of the Catholic Church; a few are Evangelicalism|evangelical Protestantism|Protestants.
