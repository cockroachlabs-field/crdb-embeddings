Welsh notably played in the Premier League for Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool, where he began his career before playing for Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City, Chester City F.C.|Chester City, Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United, Bury F.C.|Bury, Tranmere Rovers F.C.|Tranmere Rovers and Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town. In 2019 he dropped down to play at semi-professional level with Atherton Collieries F.C.|Atherton Collieries before finishing his career with Stafford Rangers F.C.|Stafford Rangers.  He was captain of the England national under-20 football team|England under-20 team, and has been Cap (football)|capped eight times for the England national under-21 football team|under-21s. He represented England U20 at 2003 FIFA World Youth Championship. ==Career==  Wavertree born Welsh, joined Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool at the age of 10. He progressed through the ranks, becoming captain of the Reserve Team in the process. Welsh trained with the Liverpool first team squad at their Melwood training ground from the middle of the 2001-02 in English football|2001–02 season. In the 2002-03 in English football|2002–03 season John Welsh played only one game for the first team&nbsp;– his début match against Ipswich Town F.C.|Ipswich Town on 4 December 2002 in a Football League Cup|League Cup fourth round tie at home&nbsp;– when he replaced Vladimír Šmicer in the 83rd minute. Also, he was amongst the substitutes for a number of UEFA|European games. His first game in Premier League|Premiership was against Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal on 4 October 2003, when he replaced Salif Diao in the 82nd minute, but he couldn't help his team avoid defeat&nbsp;– they lost 2–1, and unfortunately for Welsh, his first act was to be booked for a foul on Ray Parlour. Welsh featured in Liverpool's run to the 2005 UEFA Champions League Final, coming on as a substitute in the round of 16 away tie against Bayer Leverkusen. Liverpool would go on to win the trophy but Welsh was left out of the final match day squad.  After three starts and seven substitute appearances in all competitions for Liverpool, in August 2005, John went on a season-long loan to Football League Championship|Championship side Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City. The club were impressed, and in November 2005 agreed a deal which saw Liverpool take promising young winger Paul Anderson (footballer)|Paul Anderson in exchange. The swap took place on 4 January 2006. However, three minutes into a match against Preston North End F.C.|Preston North End on 10 March 2007, Welsh dived into a tackle with Preston's Neil Mellor, a former Liverpool teammate. Welsh was carried off on a stretcher, and it later transpired that he had broken both the tibia and fibula in his right leg, ruling him out for many months.  On 31 December 2007, Chester City F.C.|Chester City announced they would be signing Welsh on loan when the transfer window reopened the following day. He made his Chester debut on 1 January 2008, in a 2–0 home defeat to Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town, and spent the rest of the month in Chester's starting line-up before returning to Hull. He joined Carlisle United F.C.|Carlisle United on 27 October 2008 for an initial one-month loan making his debut against Hartlepool United F.C.|Hartlepool United. In March 2009, he joined Football League Two side Bury F.C.|Bury on loan and was released from Hull on 2 June 2009.  In July 2009, Welsh signed a deal with League One side Tranmere Rovers. He had a very consistent season for Tranmere, playing the holding midfield role successfully. Welsh also scored four goals in his first season at Prenton Park, including a volley in the 4–2 victory over Gillingham and a 25-yard strike in a 2–1 home victory over Brighton. The club offered him contract terms for the 2012–13 season.  In May 2012, it was announced that Welsh had signed for Preston North End on a two-year contract. He was voted Preston North End Player of the Year Award Winners|Player of the Year and Players' Player of the Year of Preston North End for the season 2012/13. On 17 December 2013, Welsh extended his contract with Preston for a further twelve months, thus keeping him at the club until the summer of 2015. Despite Tom Clarke (footballer, born 1987)|Tom Clarke taking over the captaincy for the 2014–15 season, Welsh still played a major part in getting the club promoted via the play-offs. This form was rewarded with a new two-year contract. At the end of the 2018 season he left Preston North End and was given an award for longest serving Preston player.  Welsh signed a two-year contract with EFL League Two|League Two club Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town on 25 June 2018. He was firmly installed as club Captain (association football)|captain for the 2018–19 EFL League Two|2018–19 season. Welsh left the club by mutual consent on 20 March 2019. This may have been in part due to his participation in a fracas at a bus stop in which he broke a woman's leg.  In October 2019 he joined Atherton Collieries F.C.|Atherton Collieries on a short-term contract.  It was announced in August 2020 that he had signed for Stafford Rangers F.C.|Stafford Rangers, on step 10 of the English football pyramid.
