  ""Acestrorhynchus"" ("needle jaw" ) is a genus of 14 species of Characiformes|characiform fish found only in fresh water in South America, the sole genus in the family "Acestrorhynchidae". Their greatest diversity is in the Orinoco and Amazon basins. These fish have elongated Esox|pike-like bodies and large conical teeth, adapted for predation on other types of fish. They are sometimes referred to as freshwater barracudas in the aquarium trade, although the name is used of other characins, as well. They range from 35 to 400&nbsp;mm in length. Common names are "cachorinho, cachorro, mopiye, payala, pejezorro, pez cachorro, pez zorro", pike characin, "moinge, halatawéi, halataway, dagu fisi, ueua, wayabra, zadoe," freshwater barracuda, "saicanga, branca, cajaba, cachorra magra, cadelinha "and" dentudo." ==Species== * "Acestrorhynchus abbreviatus" <small>(Edward Drinker Cope|Cope, 1878)</small> * "Acestrorhynchus altus" <small>Naércio Aquino de Menezes|Menezes, 1969</small> * "Acestrorhynchus britskii" <small>Menezes, 1969</small> * "Acestrorhynchus falcatus" <small>(Marcus Elieser Bloch|Bloch, 1794)</small> (freshwater barracuda, spotted cachorro) * "Acestrorhynchus falcirostris" <small>(Georges Cuvier|G.
