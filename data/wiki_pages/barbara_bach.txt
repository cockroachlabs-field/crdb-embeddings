     |  }} | children           = 2, including Francesca Gregorini }} "Barbara Bach, Lady Starkey" (born "Barbara Goldbach"; August 27, 1946 or 1947) is an American actress and model. She played the Bond girl Anya Amasova in The Spy Who Loved Me (film)|"The Spy Who Loved Me". She is married to former The Beatles|Beatles drummer Ringo Starr. == Early life == Bach is a native of Jackson Heights, Queens and was raised Catholic Church|Roman Catholic. She graduated from Dominican Commercial High School, an all-girls school, in 1964. The following year, she shortened her surname to Bach and began modeling professionally, appearing in catalogs and fashion magazines. == Career == Bach was one of the most sought-after faces of the 1960s, working with the Ford Models|Eileen Ford Agency in New York, appearing on catalogs and the front covers of several international fashion magazines such as "Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen" (1965 and 1966), Vogue (magazine)|"Vogue" USA (July 1966) photographed by Richard Avedon, Elle (magazine)|"ELLE" France (1966), Gioia (magazine)|"Gioia" Italy (1967–1970), and "Figurino" Brazil (1970). Her acting career started in Italy, where she played Nausicaa in "The Odyssey (1968 miniseries)|L'Odissea" in 1968, an eight-hour long TV adaptation of Homer's epic poem "The Odyssey", directed by Franco Rossi (director)|Franco Rossi and produced by Dino de Laurentiis. In 1971, Bach co-starred with two other Bond girls, Claudine Auger and Barbara Bouchet, in the mystery "Black Belly of the Tarantula" (a giallo film) and appeared in other Italian films. In 1977, Bach portrayed the Russian spy Anya Amasova in the James Bond film "The Spy Who Loved Me (film)|The Spy Who Loved Me". Bach remarked after the film that Bond is "a chauvinist pig who uses girls to shield him against bullets." The following year she appeared in the movie "Force 10 from Navarone (film)|Force 10 from Navarone". She lost a role to actress Shelley Hack when she auditioned for season four of the television series "Charlie's Angels". During an interview with Johnny Carson on May 9, 1979, she said that she lost the audition for "Charlie's Angels" because they felt she was too sophisticated in attitude and look, and thought that she was not American, even though she was born in Rosedale and grew up in Jackson Heights, both in Queens, New York City. They asked her manager if she could play an American. Bach has 28 films to her credit. She was featured in a pictorial in "Playboy" in January 1981. She also had a cameo in a September 1987 special issue on the Bond girls.  In 1991, Bach co-founded the Self Help Addiction Recovery Program (SHARP) with Pattie Boyd, the former wife of George Harrison and Eric Clapton, both of whom assisted in the venture. Bach and Ringo Starr created The Lotus Foundation, a charity with many sub-charities. == Personal life == Bach's first marriage was to Italian businessman Augusto, Count Gregorini di Savignano di Romagna. The couple had two children together, daughter Francesca Gregorini|Francesca and son Gianni, before divorcing in 1975. Bach married British musician Ringo Starr, formerly of the Beatles, at Marylebone Town Hall on April 27, 1981.  The two met in 1980, on the set of the film "Caveman (film)|Caveman" (1981). Bach struggled with alcoholism and heavy drug use and, along with her husband, Ringo Starr, checked into a rehab in 1988 for four weeks. The couple have remained sober ever since. According to the International Vegetarian Union, Bach and Starr practice vegetarianism.
